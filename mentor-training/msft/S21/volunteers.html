Volunteer Activities: Experienced Mentors

Thank you so much for volunteering to help in the training! We know participants will benefit greatly from hearing how your experience was in the pilot!

  • Friday Jan. 29 from 12:15pm-1:30pm PT
  • Friday Feb. 5 from 1:15pm-2:30pm PT

Friday Jan. 29 from 12:15pm-1:30pm PT

Model an abbreviated self-efficacy video story share and round robin. This is to kick off the first training session, introducing the program and role of story-sharing. You’ll need to prepare a story for self-efficacy.

12:15pm You’ll sign onto the zoom session 15 mins before the first training session starts. Having you arrive early will let us confirm the number of breakout groups.

When you sign on, please rename yourself and place an asterisk * in front of your first name, last initial, and pronouns if you want to be referred to with pronouns. For example:

  • *Becky P, mentor (she/her)
The asterisk will make it easier to move you into the proper breakouts.

Rename in zoom

At the bottom menu bar (in black) click on the Participants button. Then find your name in the list, click More, then rename, update your screen name and click OK.

12:30pm New mentors arrive; we welcome and offer ground rules before showing the self-efficacy video.
~12:45pm You'll facilitate a 20-25 minute breakout for ~15 participants.
  1. Ask people to mute unless they are speaking.
  2. Introduce yourself (~2 minutes). Include: your role at MSFT, why you signed up to be a mentor last fall.
  3. Explain how the story share works.
  4. Explain the format of the breakout: In the sessions, you would typically share your story before the video. You would then ask students to break out and talk in pairs, and come back and share. In this simulation, I will share my story and immediately call upon 4 people to share if they resonated with the video in what is called a “Round Robin.” I will then synthesize and reflect back. This activity is done in every single session.
  5. Share your story (2-3 mins) about self-efficacy.
  6. Invite 4-5 different participants in a selected round robin.
    X, would you be willing to share your thoughts about this video? It’s ok if you pass.
  7. Then synthesize and reflect back what you heard.
  8. There should be some open time for discussion. You can share your own reflections on the video pack and story share activity in particular. These prompts may be helpful:
    • How did sharing these videos open up conversation in the program, from your perspective?
    • Did you find that it became easier to do the share and facilitation as the sessions went on?
We’ll give you a 5 minute warning before we pull you back into the Main Room and invite participants to share their experiences.
1:30pm We will thank you, so you can sign off.

Friday Feb. 5 from 1:15pm-2:30pm PT

Respond to questions participants have about “what can go wrong.” This is during the third training session, immediately following the activity on “pivot” scenarios for cognitive flexibility. You’ll want to prepare some examples that happened during your mentoring experience.

1:15pm You’ll sign onto the zoom session, which will be under way. The session will have started at 12:30pm, and participants will be in the middle of the breakout activity on pivot prompts. Having you arrive early will let us confirm the number of breakout groups and give you time to rename yourself before they report back.

When you sign on, please rename yourself and place an asterisk * in front of your first name, last initial, and pronouns if you want to be referred to with pronouns. For example:

  • *Becky P, mentor (she/her)
The asterisk will make it easier to move you into the proper breakouts.

~1:30pm Once the report back completes, we’ll introduce you as a group and move you into breakouts.
~12:45pm You'll facilitate a 20-25 minute breakout with 1-2 other mentors from the Fall Pilot for ~30 participants.
  1. Ask people to mute unless they are speaking.
  2. You each introduce yourselves (~2 minutes). Include: your role at MSFT, why you signed up to be a mentor last fall.
  3. Explain the format of the breakout: We’ll start with one prepared question and then open it up to you all. The question we'll start with is:
    Looking back, what was the best part of mentoring with a co-mentor? How did your co-mentor help you with anything that was “unexpected” or “went wrong”?
  4. To help you get a sense of what's on their minds, ask the group: Take a minute to reflect on the pivot activity you just completed and the training thus far. Is there anything you are wondering about or worrying about that might happen in the program that you'd like to discuss today? Please use the chat to describe, but don’t press return! Once everyone has had a couple of minutes to prepare their ideas, we'll have you hit the button and we'll get started.
  5. After 2-3 minutes of wait-time, please go ahead and ask people to enter their response in the chat. Take a minute to skim over these. You can write down a few ideas of things you want to be sure to address after the prepared questions are done.
  6. Read through the chat and notice if anything stands out. Each of you can notice if any situations or topics did not arise. Take turns responding to what's been brought up, keeping an eye on time. You can share what did or did not happen in your own sessions and/or whether you shared the same concerns.
We may drop in just to listen (or suggest topics if questions seem sparse). We’ll give you a 5 minute warning before we pull you back into the Main Room and invite some of you to share out questions that came up.
2:15 - 2:30pm We will thank you, so you can sign off.
